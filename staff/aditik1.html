Biography

Aditi studied chemistry and physics in the College of Creative Studies at the University of California, Santa Barbara. While at UCSB, she conducted research with Professor Ram Seshadri in the Materials Research Laboratory.

She also previously worked at other locations including Cornell University, SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory, and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (The Molecular Foundry). Her research consisted of implementing density functional theory (DFT) and a first principles approach to study the electronic structure and properties of various extended crystalline systems such as lone pair perturbations, transparent conducting materials, multiferroics, photovoltaics, and perovskite solar cells.

Currently, Aditi's research focuses on studying two-dimensional materials by using a combination of quantum mechanical calculations and statistical/machine learning algorithms to better understand their phase change mechanisms. She also works on better implementations of tight-binding models.

Aditi is funded by the Department of Energy Computational Science Graduate Fellowship (DOE CSGF) and has also worked with Los Alamos National Laboratory on this research.

Aditi was born in the land of picturesque green rolling hills and rushing rivers, also known as Des Moines, Iowa, before moving to California. In her free time, she enjoys distance running, reading, and studying languages.
