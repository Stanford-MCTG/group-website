Biography

Yao Li is a Ph.D. candidate in Applied Physics Department at Stanford. She received her Bachelor's degree in Physics from Nanjing University in 2011. She joined Reed group in Fall 2011.

Yao Li's research focuses on simulations of two-dimensional (2D) materials to explore their potential applications in electronic devices. One example is strain engineering in monolayer materials using patterned adatom adsorption. Yao li and coworkers have shown that strains as large as 5% can be produced using this method, and their work elucidates a new method for strain engineering at the nanoscale in monolayer devices. Another project she is currently working on is structural phase transitions in 2D materials, which has vast applications in electronic devices, e.g. phase-change memory.

Yao Li likes drawing and swimming. She also enjoys watching movies and soccer matches.
