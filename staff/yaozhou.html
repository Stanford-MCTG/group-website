Biography

Yao is a graduate student in Stanford University's Department of Materials Science and Engineering.

Yao received her B.Sc. in Applied Physics from the University of Science and Technology of China in 2013, where she worked in a condensed matter computation group to develop part of an electronic structure calculation package. She also worked in a functional nanomaterials laboratory to develop surface plasmon enhanced solar cells with Ag nanoparticles.

Yao joined Prof. Evan Reedâ€™s group in the fall of 2013, and is interested in simulations of two-dimensional (2D) materials for potential electronic applications. One project she worked on is the study of atomic and molecular adsorption on monolayer MoTe2. Yao and coworkers show the potential for adsorption to induce a structural phase change between the semiconducting and metallic crystal structures of the monolayer and its alloy, potentially providing the basis for molecular sensing applications. She currently works on van der Waals interactions in layered materials.

In her free time, she enjoys music and playing cello. She also likes hiking with her friends.
