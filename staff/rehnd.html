Education

  • B.S. Engineering Physics, University of Colorado, Boulder, 2012
Biography
Danny is a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering. His research has focused on numerical methods for time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) and thermodynamics of phase transitions in 2D materials. He has also worked on developing new methods for computing electron correlation energies using ACFDT and has an interest in code design using modern (object-oriented) Fortran and coarrays in Fortran.

Danny interned at Los Alamos National Lab, where he helped develop a C++/Python interface to an existing nuclear data parser. Prior to studying at Stanford, he worked at the University of Colorado with the Physics Education Research group and the Plasma Physics group.
