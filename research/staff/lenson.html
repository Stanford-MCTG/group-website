Education

Biography

Lenson started studying the sciences at Brown University, class of 2008. He majored in materials science and engineering, with particular interest in fundamental physics and mathematics. He worked for two years in optoelectronics and quantum devices at the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), designing materials for use in single photon detectors and building familiarity with quantum mechanics. He left NIST to undertake graduate study at Stanford in 2010, and began working on computation and theory of materials with Professor Reed's group in 2011.

He is presently studying new methods for modeling chemistry in extreme environments and coherent control of molecular structures using shaped terahertz pulses.

In his free time, he likes to take long bike rides, go backpacking, soak up sunshine, and play music.
