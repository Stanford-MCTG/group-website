yaoliphy _at_ stanford.edu

Education

  • B.S. Physics, Nanjing University, China, 2011
Biography

Yao Li is a graduate student in the Applied Physics Department at Stanford University. She went to Nanjing University in 2007 to pursue a Bachelor's degree in Physics. There she has developed strong interests in the study of material. In the National Laboratory of Solid State Microstructures in Nanjing University, she has done some theoretical work on plasmon-induced enhancement of magneto-optical activity in nonmagnetic nanostructures and performed some experiments in the fabrication of periodic metallic nanohole samples under the supervision of Prof. Zhenlin Wang.

She joined Evan Reed's group in the autumn of 2011 and now she is interested in manipulating the properties of Graphene for its potential application in electronics.

In her free time, she enjoys watching movies and soccer matches. She also likes playing badminton with friends.
