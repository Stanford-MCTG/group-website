Biography

Karel-Alexander Duerloo is a Graduate Student at Stanford University's Department of Materials Science and Engineering.

Originally from Belgium, he moved to the Netherlands in 2008 to pursue a Bachelor’s degree in Applied Physics from Delft University of Technology. His focus there soon settled on solid-state physics, computational science and materials research. In 2010, he co-organized a conference on applications of acoustics, sonic as well as ultrasonic, to modern science and industry. In 2011, he developed a data mining software package that tracks the properties of a metal’s individual grains in datasets obtained from state of the art X-ray diffraction experiments at the European Synchrotron Radiation Facility (ESRF) in Grenoble, France.

Karel-Alexander received his Bachelor’s degree from Delft in 2011, with honors and distinction. He then moved to Stanford for graduate school, where he now develops an integrative approach to atomistic and continuum-scale models for 2D materials as part of his Ph.D. work.

He enjoys spending his spare time in San Francisco or on the tennis court. He also likes to teach and give public talks.
